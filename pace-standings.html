MLB Pace Standings 2026

Track The Season's MLB Pace Standings compare every team's current season win pace to their last 10, 20 and 30 game pace. The Momentum Delta shows which teams are heating up or cooling off relative to their full-season pace.

Pace Standings are updated automatically after every completed regular-season game using the official MLB Stats API. Teams with a positive Momentum Delta are outperforming their season average recently. Teams with a negative delta are underperforming.

How to read Pace Standings

Season Pace: the team's current win total projected over 162 games. L20 Pace: what their win pace would be if they played 162 games at their last-20-game rate. Momentum Delta: L20 Pace minus Season Pace. Trend labels: Surging (+20 or more), Heating Up (+10 to +19), Steady (-9 to +9), Cooling (-10 to -19), Sliding (-20 or worse).

Visit tracktheseason.com/pace-standings for full sortable standings by division, league, or trend.

What are Pace Standings?
Traditional standings reward what happened over the entire season. Pace Standings compare a team's full-season pace with how they've played recently — identifying clubs that are accelerating, cooling off, or holding steady before the standings fully reflect those changes. Momentum Delta = L20 Pace − Season Pace. Panic Score (0-99) measures how sharply a contending team's recent pace has dropped from its season pace — shown only for teams with a 75W+ season pace, since panic isn't meaningful for teams already out of contention.
