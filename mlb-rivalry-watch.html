MLB Rivalry Watch

The box score tells you who won last night. Rivalry Watch tells you which team is actually winning the season — by comparing projected win pace, year-over-year delta, and recent momentum between rival teams.

What is MLB Rivalry Watch?

Rivalry Watch compares two rival teams across the metrics that matter most for understanding a season: current projected win pace, wins gained or lost vs last year, last 10 record, and payroll efficiency. It answers the question fans argue about — not who won last night, but which team is actually tracking better across a full season.

Every rivalry page updates automatically after every completed game using the MLB Stats API.

How does Track The Season compare rival teams?

For each rivalry comparison, Track The Season calculates:

  • Current record and 162-game win pace for each team
  • Delta vs last season — wins ahead or behind last year at the same games-played point
  • Pace gap — difference in projected wins between the two teams
  • Last 10 and Last 20 records — recent momentum for each team
  • Payroll efficiency — cost per projected win for each team
  • Bragging rights — which team leads in each category

How does the Phillies vs Mets rivalry look this season?

Visit tracktheseason.com/phillies-vs-mets for the current comparison. The page shows which team leads in projected wins, which is trending better over the last 10 games, and how each team's payroll stacks up per projected win.

Are the Yankees or Red Sox having a better season?

The Yankees vs Red Sox rivalry page answers this with data. The pace comparison shows which team is tracking more wins over a full season, which is ahead of last year's pace, and whether the gap is growing or narrowing.
